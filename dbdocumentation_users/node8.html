Scope

Because all plants have the potential to be used as biofuel feedstock, we have not set any a-priori constraints on the species included in the database. In practice, the species included in the database at present reflect available data and the research interests of contributors. Trait and Yield data is provided at the level of species, with cultivar and clone information provided where available.

BETYdb contains data from intensive efforts to find data for specific species of interest as well as from previous plant trait and yield syntheses, and other databases.Most of the data currently in the database is from plant groups that are the focus of our current research (Table 2.2. These species include perennial grasses, such as Miscanthus (Miscanthus sinensis) Switchgrass (Panicum virgatum), and sugarcane (Saccgaryn spp.). BETY also includes short-rotation woody species, including poplar (Populus spp.) and willow (Salix spp.) and a group of species that are being evaluated at the energy farm as novel woody crops (table 22).
